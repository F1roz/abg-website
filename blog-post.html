5 things no one tells you before your first Global Volunteer trip

A first-hand look at what actually happens between accepting your exchange and landing back home — from a returned AIESEC Bangladesh exchange participant.

1. The waiting is the hardest part

Between getting accepted and actually leaving, there's a stretch of paperwork, visa appointments and overthinking that nobody warns you about. Use this section to describe what that waiting period actually felt like, and what helped.

2. Your host LC becomes your safety net

Talk about the local AIESEC team that received you abroad — airport pickup, the buddy system, and how quickly strangers started to feel like a second team.

3. The project rarely goes exactly to plan

Be honest about what changed once you arrived — schedules shifting, language barriers, or the project brief evolving once you actually met the community you were working with.

  • Pack less than you think you need — you can buy almost everything there.
  • Say yes to the detours; some of the best memories aren't on the itinerary.
  • Keep a short daily journal — you'll want it when you're back.

Reverse culture shock is real. Use this section to talk about what it was like returning to Dhaka, and how the experience changed how you see your own city and studies.
