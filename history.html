How it started

A handful of students with one belief

In 1997, a small group of pioneering university students came together in Bangladesh with a shared belief: that young people, given the right platform, could lead real change long before they ever entered the workforce. That belief became AIESEC in Bangladesh — the country's member entity of the world's largest youth-run organisation, part of a global network active since 1948.

What began with a handful of members and a single Local Committee has since grown into a nationwide platform for leadership development, international exchange and youth-driven impact.
